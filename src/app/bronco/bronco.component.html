The Story of my Bronco

The beginnings

In middle school, I purchased a 1979 Ford Bronco as a car that I could drive when I became of driving age. I really loved the lines of the truck, and it is also one of the most capable off roading vehicles that Ford ever made. It is also highly desirable due to the fact that 1979 was the last year that the Bronco was manufactured with a solid front axle, a feature that is widely valued by the off-roading community. After getting the Bronco, I quickly learned my way around the engine. I started with smaller things, such as oil pan removal, and worked my way up to bigger things, such as replacing the carburetor and the rear main seal. The tailgate of the truck also had electrical problems, so I also learned how to do automotive electrical work.

Trouble

After working out many of the mechanical issues with the truck, I took the Bronco on the highway one day. During this trip, I experienced a loss of power, followed by a loud clunk and the engine abruptly shutting off while I was traveling at around 60 miles per hour. Luckily, I was able to coast to a nearby off ramp and I was able to call a tow truck to return the Bronco home. After assessing the damage to the engine, I found that the truck had thrown a connecting rod. I decided to do a full engine swap and transmission rebuild.

The rebuild

Currently, I have the transmission rebuilt, and the engine has been refreshed as well. I'm currently working on refreshing the transfer case. After I have the transfer case done, I will be looking to reassemble the drivetrain and put it back into the Bronco.
